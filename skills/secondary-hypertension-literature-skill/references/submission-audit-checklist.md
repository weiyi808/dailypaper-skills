## 2. Secondary Hypertension–Specific Audit

### Diagnostic Criteria Consistency
- [ ] The diagnostic criteria for the secondary hypertension subtype are explicitly stated
- [ ] Criteria are consistent across included studies OR heterogeneity across criteria is noted as a limitation
- [ ] The review does not use the term "diagnosis" without specifying how diagnosis was made in each study

### Statistical Integrity
- [ ] All effect estimates include 95% CI (not just p-values)
- [ ] I² is reported with N studies and N participants
- [ ] Prediction interval is reported if I² > 25%
- [ ] Subgroup analyses are labeled as pre-specified or post-hoc
- [ ] Sensitivity analyses (e.g., excluding high-RoB studies) are reported
- [ ] Meta-analysis software and version are cited

### GRADE Alignment
- [ ] GRADE certainty is stated for each primary outcome in the SoF table
- [ ] Downgrade reasons are specified in footnotes
- [ ] Conclusions do not claim higher certainty than GRADE warrants
- [ ] "High certainty" is not claimed without RCT evidence + low RoB

### Language Audit
- [ ] No "proves" or "demonstrates causation" for observational evidence
- [ ] No "significantly" without an explicit statistical test (or clarify "clinically significant")
- [ ] No "our results confirm" — use "are consistent with" or "support"
- [ ] Surrogate outcomes (BP reduction) are not presented as equivalent to clinical outcomes (CV events)

## 3. Reference Integrity Check

- [ ] All cited DOIs resolve (spot-check ≥ 10 references)
- [ ] No references from predatory journals (check DOAJ / Beall's List if uncertain)
- [ ] Landmark trials are cited correctly (CORAL, HIPARCO, Endocrine Society guidelines)
- [ ] Most recent guidelines are cited (ESC/ESH 2023 or Endocrine Society most recent version)
- [ ] Reference format matches target journal style (Vancouver/numbered or AMA)

## 4. Reviewer Simulation — Anticipate These Critiques

### Critique 1: Diagnostic Heterogeneity
> "The included studies used different diagnostic criteria for [subtype]. This makes pooling unreliable."

**Pre-emptive response in manuscript:** State criteria heterogeneity explicitly in Results (study characteristics table). Address it in Discussion limitations. Run sensitivity analysis restricted to studies using the most current guideline criteria. Note this in GRADE indirectness domain.

---

### Critique 2: Small Studies / High Imprecision
> "The sample sizes are too small. The 95% CI is too wide to draw conclusions."

**Pre-emptive response:** Report prediction interval. Apply GRADE imprecision downgrade explicitly. Conclude conservatively. In Discussion: "Adequately powered RCTs with [N] participants are needed to detect a clinically meaningful difference of [X mmHg / RR Y]."

---

### Critique 3: Open-Label Bias / RoB
> "All surgical vs. medical comparisons are open-label. Blinding was not possible, introducing performance and detection bias."

**Pre-emptive response:** Acknowledge in RoB Results section. Downgrade GRADE for risk of bias. State it explicitly in limitations. Consider: "objective outcomes (BP measured by ABPM) were less susceptible to detection bias than subjective outcomes."

---

### Critique 4: Short Follow-Up
> "Follow-up of [X months] is insufficient to assess long-term cardiovascular outcomes."

**Pre-emptive response:** State this in the protocol (outcomes section). Report available long-term data if any. In Discussion: "Long-term cardiovascular endpoint data beyond [X months] were unavailable in most studies. This represents a critical evidence gap."

---

### Critique 5: No PROSPERO Registration
> "The absence of prospective registration raises concerns about selective reporting."

**Pre-emptive response (if applicable):** "The protocol was not registered prospectively because [specific reason, e.g., the review was initiated as a clinical need assessment]. To reduce selective outcome reporting, we defined primary outcomes before data extraction and report all pre-specified outcomes regardless of statistical significance."

## 5. Cover Letter Draft Template

```
[Date]

Dear [Editor Name / Editor-in-Chief],

We submit for consideration the manuscript entitled "[Full Title]" for publication
in [Journal Name].

Clinical relevance: Secondary hypertension due to [subtype] affects approximately [X%]
of hypertensive patients and carries [higher cardiovascular risk / treatment-reversible
outcomes / diagnostic challenges]. Despite this burden, no systematic review has
[specific gap this review fills].

Contribution: This [systematic review / meta-analysis] provides [first / most comprehensive /
updated] synthesis of [topic] in [N] studies encompassing [N] patients. We applied GRADE
certainty assessment and found [summary of main finding and certainty level].

Fit with [Journal Name]: [One sentence on why this topic fits the journal's scope —
e.g., JAMA's focus on actionable clinical evidence, or Hypertension's focus on mechanistic
and clinical hypertension science].

Originality: This work has not been published and is not under consideration elsewhere.
All authors have approved the submission.

We appreciate your consideration and look forward to your response.

Sincerely,
[Corresponding Author Name]
[Institution, Country]
[Email]
```
