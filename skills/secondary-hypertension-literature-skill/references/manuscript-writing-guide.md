# Manuscript Writing Guide — JAMA-Style Secondary Hypertension Reviews

Load this module during Stage 6. Use templates for each section. Adapt to the specific subtype.

---

## Front Matter

### Title Rules
- Specific: name the subtype, not just "secondary hypertension"
- Informative: state the review type and key question
- No abbreviations in the title
- Preferred format: "[Topic]: A Systematic Review [and Meta-analysis]"

**Examples:**
- "Adrenalectomy Versus Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists in Primary Aldosteronism: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis"
- "Prevalence of Secondary Hypertension in Patients With Resistant Hypertension: A Systematic Review"
- "Effect of CPAP on Blood Pressure in Obstructive Sleep Apnea With Resistant Hypertension: A Meta-analysis of Randomized Controlled Trials"

---

### Key Points (3 Bullets — JAMA Format)

```
Key Points

Question  [One-sentence clinical question the review addresses]

Findings  [Main quantitative or qualitative finding with key number if applicable]

Meaning   [Clinical implication in one sentence; conservative, not promotional]
```

**Example (PA adrenalectomy):**
```
Key Points

Question  What is the comparative effectiveness of adrenalectomy versus mineralocorticoid
receptor antagonists for blood pressure control in patients with primary aldosteronism?

Findings  In this systematic review of [N] studies including [N] patients,
adrenalectomy was associated with a significantly greater reduction in systolic blood
pressure (mean difference, X mmHg; 95% CI, X to X) and higher rates of biochemical cure
compared with medical therapy, with moderate certainty of evidence.

Meaning   For patients with unilateral primary aldosteronism, adrenalectomy appears to
offer superior blood pressure outcomes, though long-term cardiovascular event data remain limited.
```

### Structured Abstract Template

```
Importance
[Why this question matters clinically; 1–2 sentences on burden and gap]

Objective
[State the specific review objective, matching the PICO/PECO]

Evidence Review
[Databases searched, date range, study designs included, number of studies and patients,
RoB tool used, synthesis method, GRADE framework]

Findings
[N studies (N patients) were included. [Main quantitative result with 95% CI and certainty rating].
[Key secondary finding if important]. Certainty of evidence was [rating] for the primary outcome.]

Conclusions and Relevance
[Conservative interpretation matching GRADE certainty. Clinical implication without overclaiming.
State what further research is needed.]
```

## Introduction Template

```
Paragraph 1 — Burden
Secondary hypertension accounts for approximately [X%] of all hypertension cases and is
particularly prevalent in patients with resistant or difficult-to-control hypertension.
[Specific subtype] affects [prevalence estimate] of hypertensive patients and is associated
with [adverse outcomes: e.g., higher cardiovascular risk, target organ damage, resistant HTN].

Paragraph 2 — Knowledge Gap
Despite [clinical importance], the evidence base for [intervention / diagnostic approach]
in [specific subtype] remains heterogeneous and inconclusive. [Prior reviews or guidelines:
cite 1–2; state their limitations — e.g., older search date, different PICO, no GRADE].
No systematic review has specifically examined [gap this review fills].

Paragraph 3 — Objective
This systematic review [and meta-analysis] aimed to [restate review objective verbatim from PICO].
```

## Methods Template

```
Protocol and Registration
This systematic review was conducted in accordance with the Preferred Reporting Items for
Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses (PRISMA) 2020 statement. [The protocol was registered
in PROSPERO (CRD ID: XXXXX) prior to data extraction / was not registered because (reason).]

Eligibility Criteria
We included [study designs] reporting [outcomes] in [population with specific secondary
hypertension subtype confirmed by (diagnostic criteria / guideline version)].
We excluded [exclusion criteria with rationale].

Search Strategy
We searched [databases] from [date] to [date] using a combination of controlled vocabulary
and free-text terms. The complete search strategy is provided in the Supplement.

Study Selection
Titles and abstracts were screened by [two independent reviewers], with disagreements resolved
by [consensus / third reviewer]. Full texts were assessed for eligibility using pre-specified criteria.
Full-text exclusion reasons were recorded for PRISMA reporting.

Data Extraction
[Two reviewers] independently extracted data using a standardized form. Extracted items included:
[list key extraction domains from data-extraction-sheet.md]. Discrepancies were resolved by consensus.

Risk of Bias Assessment
Risk of bias was assessed using [RoB 2 for RCTs / ROBINS-I for non-randomized studies /
NOS for observational studies / QUADAS-2 for diagnostic accuracy studies].

Statistical Analysis
[State: random-effects or fixed-effects model, and justification].
We quantified heterogeneity using I², τ², and the prediction interval.
Publication bias was assessed using [funnel plot inspection + Egger's test, if ≥ 10 studies].
Pre-specified subgroup analyses included: [list].
All analyses were performed using [RevMan 5.4 / R metafor package / STATA].

Certainty of Evidence
Certainty of evidence was assessed using the GRADE framework for each pre-specified primary outcome.
```

## Results Template

```
Literature Search
The database search identified [N] records. After removing [N] duplicates, [N] records were
screened. Of these, [N] were assessed for full-text eligibility, and [N] studies met inclusion
criteria. [Figure 1: PRISMA 2020 flow diagram.]

Study Characteristics
[N] studies including [N] patients were included. [Describe: study designs, countries, settings,
sample sizes, follow-up durations, diagnostic criteria for secondary HTN subtype, key baseline characteristics.]
[Table 1: Characteristics of included studies.]

Risk of Bias
[Summarize RoB findings across studies: proportion at low / some concern / high risk.
Note the most common bias domain. Table or figure as appropriate.]

[Primary Outcome]
[N] studies ([N] patients) reported [primary outcome]. The pooled [effect measure] was
[X] (95% CI, X to X; I² = X%; prediction interval, X to X; [N] studies; [certainty rating]).
[If I² > 50%: Sources of heterogeneity included [subgroup analysis findings].] [Figure: forest plot.]

[Secondary Outcomes — one paragraph each]

Certainty of Evidence
[Present Summary of Findings table. State reason for each downgrade. Avoid tables
with empty certainty cells.]
```

## Discussion Template

```
Paragraph 1 — Summary of Main Finding
This systematic review [and meta-analysis] of [N] studies found [restate main finding
with effect size and certainty]. This is clinically relevant because [brief interpretation].

Paragraph 2 — Comparison to Prior Evidence
Our findings are [consistent with / diverge from] previous reviews and guidelines.
[Cite 1–2 prior reviews; explain why results differ if they do: different PICO,
more studies, updated search, better RoB assessment.]
The [landmark trial / guideline / meta-analysis] (Author, Year) reported [X], which
[supports / contradicts] our findings [because…].

Paragraph 3 — Subgroup and Sensitivity Analyses
Pre-specified subgroup analyses suggested that [finding], though these analyses
should be interpreted as hypothesis-generating rather than confirmatory.
[Note if exploratory analyses were conducted post-hoc; label them as such.]

Paragraph 4 — Limitations
This review has several limitations. First, [most important limitation — e.g.,
high heterogeneity in diagnostic criteria across studies]. Second, [RoB issues —
e.g., most studies were open-label due to the nature of the intervention].
Third, [imprecision — e.g., only X RCTs with small sample sizes; 95% CI wide].
Fourth, [indirectness — e.g., surrogate BP outcomes; limited long-term cardiovascular data].
These limitations reduce the certainty of evidence for [specific outcome] to [GRADE level].

Paragraph 5 — Implications
For clinical practice: [Conservative recommendation aligned with GRADE certainty].
For research: [Specific gaps — e.g., "Adequately powered RCTs with long-term
CV event endpoints are needed"].
```

## Conclusion Template

```
[N-word concise conclusion — 2–3 sentences maximum]

In this systematic review, [restate main finding with certainty level].
These findings [support / suggest / are insufficient to support] [clinical recommendation].
[Long-term randomized trial data / standardized diagnostic criteria / larger studies] are
needed to [specify next research priority].
```

## JAMA Style Constraints

- Word count: structured abstract ≤ 350 words; manuscript ≤ 3500 words (vary by article type)
- Figure limit: typically 4–6; forest plots count as figures
- Table limit: typically 3–5; SoF table is mandatory for systematic reviews
- References: cite numbered in order of appearance; Vancouver style
- Abbreviations: define at first use; avoid in title and Key Points
- Supplement: full search strategy, extraction forms, sensitivity analyses, additional figures
- Statistical reporting: always "mean difference, X mmHg (95% CI, X to X)" — not "p < 0.05"
- Do NOT use "significantly" unless referring to statistical significance with an explicit test
- Do NOT use "prove", "demonstrate causation", "confirm" for observational evidence
